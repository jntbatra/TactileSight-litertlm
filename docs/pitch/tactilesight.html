TactileSight01 / 03

Wearable navigation for the blind

A cane finds the floor.
It cannot answer a question.

A white cane reaches about a metre and tells you only that something is there. It cannot tell you the gap on your left is a doorway, that the shape ahead is a person rather than a pillar, or what the sign above the door says. Ask a phone assistant instead and it will describe a photograph, and guess at how far away things are. A confident wrong distance is worse than no distance when you are walking.

People43m blind worldwide, plus 295 m with moderate-to-severe vision impairment.
Denied access1bn of the 2.5 bn who need assistive products cannot get them. Two thirds of users pay out of pocket.
The devices that do more$4.5k–$11.7k Priced as medical devices, most with a subscription and a cloud round-trip on top.

TactileSight is two answers on one body. A headband turns depth into vibration, so you feel the shape of the room continuously. A single button on that band asks the phone what is there, answered on-device, in your own language, with every distance measured rather than guessed.
